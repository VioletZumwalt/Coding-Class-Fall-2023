How to Paint a Hat

A basic guide

What do you need?

  • Paint Set
  • Fabric Paint
  • A Hat (preferably White)
  • a safe place to put it
  • Possible Sewing Materials (hand or machine)
  • Fabrics (if sewing)
  • A glass of water

Material Gathering

All of the Materials listed above can be found in most local stores. Sewing Materials are not mandatory, but they can help, if you wish to sew things onto the hat as well. Such as horns.
Any store can be a viable option, but I personally recoomend Michaels. This is their listing for their hats, and one of the cheaper options.

Painting

Having a clean workspace is best when painting. A layer below your hat will prevent staining, but nothing is truly required. Be sure to have your glass of water on hand.

IMPORTANT. Light paints should be applied before dark paints. Figure out your layering for complex ideas.

Step What we're doing
Step 1 Be sure that you're using fabric paint and not acrylic. If you have any patterns in mind, you should sketch them out on a piece of paper, rather than starting on the hat. That way, you'll be more confident in what your final project will be
Step 2 Star with the front, above the brim. Paint with a wide brush, and get an even layer. Be careful not to touch it, as finger prints will appear on the paint.
Step 3 Work further, and paint around the hat. It is recomended that you have the hat on a stable surface, or hold it so that it doesn't move too much. Don't paint on the bottom where it touches the ground yet. This will be done later to ensure it doesn't smudge.
Step 4 Now that the main section has dried, paint the brim of the hat. Continue to be careful with even strokes.
Step 5 Now that that has dried, paint the bottom part of the brim. You'll want to do this seperately so that the hat can rest.
Step 6 Examine the now dry hat. Are there any areas where the paint looks thin? Weird layering? This is where you'll be doing touch up work.
Step 7 If you have any patching plans, this is the time to do it! Be sure to get some measurements and position testing done to make sure you're happy before sewing.

CONGRATS!

Now you have yourself a painted hat! If you need visual instruction, here's a video guide for a patterned hat.
